Roast Turkey

Description

Turkey meat, commonly referred to as just turkey, is the meat from turkeys, typically domesticated turkeys, but also wild turkeys.

Nothing screams "Thanksgiving" quite like a picturesque roast turkey. Believe it or not, an impossibly gorgeous holiday table of your dreams is within reach â€” learn exactly how to roast a turkey with this easy recipe.

Ingredients

  • Turkey
  • Stuffing
  • Butter
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Turkey stock

Steps

  1. 1. Prepare the turkey - Remove the turkey neck and giblets. Pat the turkey dry with paper towels, then place it (breast-side up) on a rack in a roasting pan.
  2. 2. Stuff and season the turkey: Fill the cavity with stuffing. Rub the skin with butter, then season with salt and pepper. Pour two cups of stock into the roasting pan and loosely tent the turkey with foil.
  3. 3. Roast the turkey: Roast, basting with stock every 30 minutes, for about two and half hours. Add more stock as the drippings evaporate. Remove the foil and continue roasting until a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the thigh reads 165 degrees F.
